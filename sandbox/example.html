Draft a shared community story

Writers co-create a neighborhood zine that uplifts local voices.

Today you will remix interview notes and sensory details collected around campus into a collaborative feature article.

Essential question

How can we blend our perspectives to publish a story that feels authentic to everyone involved?

Key vocabulary

  • angle
  • voice
  • revision cycle
  • fact-check

Study mentor texts

Skim two short community spotlights and look for moves that make the writing feel grounded and collaborative.

Explore the text

  • Highlight sentences where the writer weaves in direct quotes from neighbors.
  • Circle phrases that help readers visualize the setting.
  • Underline transitions that show how the team worked together.

What do you notice?

  • What narrative moves make each source's contribution feel distinct?
  • How do the writers balance storytelling with factual accuracy?

Sample language

  • "As the mural took shape, Maya paused to invite feedback from each passerby."
  • "Together the trio mapped questions on sticky notes before the interview began."

Draft the feature

Work in writing teams to braid interview excerpts, descriptive detail, and narration into a publishable draft.

Gather your materials

  • Interview transcripts
  • Team outline
  • Revision checklist
  • Shared document

Make together

  1. Choose a working title and list the story beats you want to cover.
  2. Divide lead, body, and conclusion paragraphs across the team and draft simultaneously.
  3. Pause every 7 minutes to read aloud a favorite sentence and collect feedback.

Share your work

  • Publish to the class zine site
  • Record an audio version for the community bulletin
  • Pin printed copies for a hallway gallery walk

Reflect on our drafting sprint

  • Which sentence from a teammate inspired a new idea for you today?
  • Where did we combine evidence and narrative most effectively?
  • What is one revision move we will try before publishing?
