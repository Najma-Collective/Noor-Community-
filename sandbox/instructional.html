Decode the JSON brief

Work with a partner to inspect the sample brief and locate the fields that power each slide region.

Explore the text

  • Match agenda beats to the layouts they trigger in the sandbox renderer.
  • Highlight where overlays, icons, and vocab lists live inside the data object.

What do you notice?

  • Which fields fall back to archetype defaults when left empty?
  • How does the brief capture accessibility copy like alt-text?

Sample language

  • "layout": "topic-introduction"
  • "overlayOpacity": 72
  • "gallery": [{ "image": "..." }]
  • "prompts": ["Add reflection prompts to guide learners."]

Customise your deck

Teams remix the generated deck by swapping imagery, updating overlays, and fine-tuning copy in the JSON before rerunning the CLI.

Gather your materials

  • Generated HTML deck for reference
  • JSON brief draft with comments
  • Shared Pexels key for fresh media pulls

Make together

  1. Choose one slide to personalise and identify the relevant JSON fields.
  2. Adjust copy, icons, and overlays, then rerun the generator.
  3. Document the before/after result and note any styling surprises.

Share your work

  • Drop screenshots of the refreshed slide in the team chat.
  • Record a quick loom walking through the updates you made.
