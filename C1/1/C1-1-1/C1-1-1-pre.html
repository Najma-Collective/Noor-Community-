Pre-Class Worksheet: Evaluating NGO Project Proposals

Complete this self-paced sequence to prepare for tomorrow's project evaluation meeting. You will explore the model dialogue, analyse key vocabulary, practise distancing language, and organise your recommendations for the Jericho Digital Heritage Initiative.

Model Dialogue: Reviewing the Jericho Digital Heritage Initiative

Read the conversation between Salma and Kareem, project officers in Ramallah who are preparing feedback for tomorrow's evaluation panel.

Salma: Thanks for jumping on this call, Kareem. We need to finalise our notes on the Jericho Digital Heritage Initiative before tomorrow's panel.

Kareem: Of course. At first glance, the goal seems ambitious but achievable. The archive could be highly viable if we leverage the university partnership.

Salma: I agree. There appear to be strong community stakeholders, especially the elders who will contribute oral histories.

Kareem: Still, I'm slightly concerned about sustainability. The budget covers two years, yet it might not include long-term hosting costs.

Salma: Right. We might need to request a maintenance plan. Another potential constraint is the 18-month timeline if any permits are delayed.

Kareem: On the positive side, the project could generate measurable impact by training local youth as technicians.

Salma: That's a strong point. If they address those shortcomings, I'd feel comfortable recommending a pilot phase.

Kareem: Agreed. Let's highlight the strengths, note the funding constraint, and ask for a monitoring plan in our feedback.
