Pre-task B · Semi-controlled Practice · 7 minutes

Analyzing the Negotiation

Dialogue insight check

Select the communicative function for each highlighted phrase.

Dana: Hi Kareem, thanks for meeting. I've reviewed the partner feedback. It’s tough. If we had anticipated the connectivity issues in the West Bank hubs, we would be in a better position now.

Kareem: I know, Dana. Hindsight is 20/20. They are suggesting we change our entire delivery platform. If we were to change suppliers, we would need to have a good contingency plan. It could delay us by months.

Dana: Exactly. I agree, but we need to think about the long-term implications of a major pivot. We can't afford to get this wrong.

Kareem: Okay, let's find a way forward. I think if we just focused on the curriculum, we could find some common ground. Perhaps we can offer more offline resources as a trade-off?

Dana: That's a good starting point. Perhaps we can make a small concession on the timeline to allow for that development?

Kareem: I think that's a reasonable compromise. I’m glad we talked this through. If we hadn't scheduled this call, we might have reached a deadlock.

Pre-task D · Restrictive Practice · 7 minutes

Pronunciation: Listening for Intonation

Activity 1 · Perception

Listen to each sentence. Is the intonation (rising on the “if” clause, falling on the result) correct?

  • If we had invested more in outreach (➚), we would have more applicants now (➘).

  • We'd be in a much stronger position (➘) if we had won that grant (➚).

  • If I had known that (➘), I would have prepared differently (➚).

  • The team would be less stressed (➘) if the report had been finished on time (➚).

  • If the partners had agreed earlier (➚), we wouldn't be having this meeting now (➘).

  • This whole problem wouldn't exist (➚) if they had listened to our advice (➘).

Pre-task D · Freer Practice · 6 minutes

30-Second Problem Analysis

1 minute to prepare, then take turns presenting for 30 seconds.

Problem cards

  • Card A: We overspent the marketing budget by 50%.
  • Card B: Our project app has very negative reviews on the app store.
  • Card C: A key member of our team has just resigned.

Speaking goals

  1. State the problem clearly.
  2. Use one mixed conditional to explain cause + result.
  3. Deliver with clear intonation (➚ in “if” clause, ➘ in result).
Main Task

Task

Task · 12 minutes

Your Goal: Choose a Pivot

“While we are impressed with the technical skills the participants are gaining, we are concerned about the project's sustainability and the risk of it creating dependency... We require you to present a strategic pivot that addresses these long-term implications.”

Project

Code from Home — a digital literacy project for youth in Bethlehem, run with an NGO partner in Amman.

Funder Concern

Fear of an extractive model that teaches skills without building sustainable local economic structures.

Pivot 1 · Freelancer Incubator

Shift the final three months to freelance skills (finding clients, bidding, invoicing, marketing). Pair graduates with Palestinian and Jordanian mentors.

  • Pros: Addresses employment directly; builds practical, marketable skills.
  • Cons: Requires new paid mentors; reduces advanced coding time.

Pivot 2 · Social Enterprise Model

Launch an in-house agency staffed by top graduates to deliver paid projects for local businesses, generating revenue for sustainability.

  • Pros: Creates jobs and revenue stream; offers real-world experience.
  • Cons: High risk; requires business management expertise.

Pivot 3 · Advanced Specialisation Track

Allow students to specialise in fields like mobile development or cybersecurity. Co-design with a Gulf tech partner who may hire top graduates.

  • Pros: Aligns skills with market demand; increases job placement potential.
  • Cons: May exclude lower-level students; depends heavily on a corporate partner.

Discuss pros/cons, use mixed conditionals, and agree on one pivot to recommend to the funder.

Reporting

Reporting to the Funder

Each group's Project Lead presents a 2-minute summary to the Funder's Representatives.

Include in your report

  • The pivot you chose.
  • How it addresses the funder's concerns about dependency.
  • One mixed conditional sentence that supports your recommendation.

Delivery tips

  • Highlight stakeholder benefits first.
  • Use data or examples from earlier discussions.
  • Close with a confident invitation for questions.
