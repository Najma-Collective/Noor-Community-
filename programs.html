Our Vision

We create welcoming classrooms where refugee learners explore new ideas, practice emerging skills, and gain the courage to lead. Each program is designed to lower barriers, foster connection, and amplify learner voice.

Trauma-informed facilitation

Educators design rituals of care, mindfulness breaks, and celebration.

Responsive curriculum

Projects align with learner goals—from university essays to community podcasts.

Community partnerships

Local colleges, libraries, and employers co-create experiential learning.

Learning in Motion

From storytelling circles to campus tours, our programs weave together experiences that build confidence and lasting friendships.

What We Do

Noor Community programs integrate trauma-informed facilitation, localized resources, and community partnerships. Our teams co-design curriculum with learners, ensuring every session is responsive to their goals and lived experiences.

  • Language fluency for academic and everyday contexts.
  • Critical thinking and research skills for higher education.
  • Holistic mentoring that addresses wellbeing and belonging.

Program Pillars

  1. Groundwork: orientation, language diagnostics, and goal setting.
  2. Practice: collaborative projects, feedback loops, and cultural exchange.
  3. Launch: showcase events, scholarship navigation, and mentor support.
