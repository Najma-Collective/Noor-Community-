About Noor Community

We are an educator-led collective supporting refugee learners through trauma-informed instruction, peer mentorship, and community-building initiatives.

12+ Years of service
450 Learners guided
60 Volunteer educators

Our Vision

Every learner deserves to feel seen, encouraged, and equipped to pursue their aspirations. We cultivate learning environments where multilingual identities are celebrated and where students can reimagine their futures with confidence.

  • Community-rooted pedagogy that affirms culture and lived experience.
  • Holistic care that centers wellbeing, family engagement, and access.
  • Leadership pathways that amplify youth voice in public forums.

Collective Story

Noor Community grew from volunteer-led study circles into a coordinated network of teachers, counselors, and student leaders across multiple cities.

Our Story

Noor Community began with a handful of educators responding to urgent language needs in local refugee resettlement centers. We now steward year-round programs that blend academic rigor with deep relational care.

  1. Launching English Immersion to welcome newly arrived youth.
  2. Creating the Writing Lab to uplift personal narratives and scholarship voices.
  3. Building the EAP Bridge to connect learners with higher education systems.
  4. Expanding mentorship with alumni coaches, career panels, and wellbeing resources.

How We Work

Our curriculum design and learner support approach center three pillars: collaborative pedagogy, culturally sustaining practice, and holistic wellbeing. We design experiences that welcome family voices, leverage community expertise, and reduce barriers to participation.

  • Language justice through translanguaging and accessible resources.
  • Academic readiness through scaffolded projects and feedback loops.
  • Career exploration via mentoring, internships, and storytelling.
