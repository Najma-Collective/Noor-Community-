Conversation Class

Work, Travel, & Your Future

Let's talk about careers and adventures.

Today's Goals

Talk About Work Experience

Use past simple and present perfect to describe your career.

Give Travel Advice

Use "should", "must", and "have to" for recommendations.

Connect Ideas

Combine vocabulary about work and travel in one conversation.

Let's Talk!

Work and Travel

1. Have you ever travelled for work or for an interview?

2. If you could get a promotion anywhere in the world, where would you have to travel?

3. What qualifications should a person have for a job in the tourism industry?

Activity 1

Combining Our Words

Let's use words from both topics in the same sentence.

For example:

To get a promotion, your qualifications must include experience with creating an itinerary.

I need to make a booking for my accommodation before my job interview.

Activity 1: Your Turn!

In pairs, create a short story or dialogue.

Your story must include:

  • At least two words about Travel (itinerary, booking, accommodation, excursion)
  • At least two words about Work (résumé, interview, promotion, qualifications)

Activity 2

Talking About Experience

Let's use the present perfect for life experience and the past simple for specific times.

"I have worked in three companies." (Life experience)
"I worked in a restaurant in 2015." (Specific time)

Your Turn! Discuss:

1. Talk about a job you have had in your life.
2. Have you ever had a difficult job interview? What happened?

Giving Advice

Activity 3: Role-Play!

Use modals to give travel advice.

Role-Play Scenario:

Student A: You are planning your first trip abroad and you are nervous. Ask for advice.

Student B: You are an experienced traveller. Give advice. Tell them one thing they must do, one thing they should do, and one thing they don't have to do.

Activity 4

A Good Problem!

Your friend has a promotion, but the new job is in another country! They have never lived abroad and need your advice.

Your Task:

In your group, brainstorm a list of 3-4 pieces of advice for your friend. Use words like:

"You should start looking for accommodation."
"You must get a visa."

Main Task: Let's Role-Play!

Scenario: A Career Counseling Session

This is a great chance to practice a full conversation!

Part 1: The Conversation

In pairs, have a conversation. Follow your roles:

Student A: The Counselor

Your goal is to give good advice. Interview the client about their past experience using the present perfect. Then, give advice using should/must.

"What work have you done before?"

Student B: The Client

Your dream is to get a job in the travel industry, but you have no experience. Answer the counselor's questions and ask for advice.

"I have never worked in a hotel."

Main Task

Part 2: Report Back

Join another pair to make a group of four.

Tell your new group about the person you just met.

"This is Ahmed. He wants to work in tourism. He has never worked in a hotel, so I told him he should try to get some experience."

Let's Reflect!

Think about your learning today.

Was it more challenging to talk about your past experience or give advice?

What is one new vocabulary word you want to remember?

How confident do you feel using 'should' and 'must' now? (1-5)
