A1 English Lesson

My Family, My Things

Talk about people and possessions.

Today's Goals

Talk About Family

Use words like brother, sister, mother, and father.

Talk About Possessions

Use words like laptop, keys, notebook, and phone.

Connect People & Things

Say things like "This is my brother's laptop."

Let's Talk!

Family and Work

1. Does your mother or father work in an office? What's on their desk?

2. Do you have a picture of your family on your desk or phone?

3. What is one important possession for your brother or sister?

Activity 1

Connecting Our Words

Let's combine our vocabulary about family and possessions.

For example:

"My brother has a new laptop."

"My mother's keys are on the table."

"I have a picture of my daughter in my wallet."

Activity 1: Your Turn!

With your partner, create a short story about a family.

Your story must include:

  • Three family words (e.g., father, sister, son)
  • Three object words (e.g., phone, notebook, keys)

Activity 2

Whose is it?

We use possessives to show who owns something.

This is my pen.
That is Fatima's notebook.
Her notebook is blue.
My brother's laptop is new.

Show and Tell

Activity 2: Your Turn!

In your group, talk about three objects around you.

For example:

"This is my notebook."

"This is my sister's pen. Her pen is red."

Activity 3

Asking Questions

Let's practice asking about a fictional family with "Do you have...?" and "Does he/she have...?"

A: "Do they have a son?"

B: "Yes, they do."

A: "Does the mother have a new laptop?"

B: "No, she doesn't."

Choose a Family

Family A: The Saleems

Adel (Father): Has a big office, has a blue car.

Salma (Mother): Has a new laptop, has a red notebook.

Omar (Son): Has a new phone, has two keys.

Family B: The Khalids

Yousef (Father): Has a black wallet, has a small garden.

Layla (Mother): Has a new bag, has a green pen.

Fatima (Daughter): Has a white cat, has many books.

Family C: The Najjars

Ibrahim (Father): Has a big family, has an old car.

Noura (Mother): Has a brother in Bethlehem, has a nice desk.

Ali (Son): Has a black notebook, has a new bicycle.

Activity 3: Your Turn!

  1. Student A: Choose a family from the last slide (A, B, or C). Don't tell your partner!
  2. Student B: Ask questions to find out about the family.

Example Questions:

"Does the father have a car?"
"Does Salma have a new laptop?"
"Do they have a son?"

Activity 4

A Problem to Solve!

Read the problem below. What does your friend need?

The Problem:

Your friend is starting a new course online. He needs an office at home. He has a desk, but he has nothing else! He doesn't have a lot of money.

Brainstorm!

Activity 4: Your Turn!

In your group, make a list of things your friend needs. How can he get them?

For example:

"He needs a laptop. Does his father have an old one?"

"Does his sister have a spare pen? He can use her pen."

Main Task: Let's Role-Play!

Scenario: Your Desk at a New Job

This is a great chance to practice a full conversation!

Part 1: The Conversation

In pairs, have a conversation. Follow your roles:

Student A

You are showing your new colleague (Student B) your desk. You have a photo of your family on the desk.

Student B

You are the new colleague. Ask Student A questions about the objects on the desk and about the family photo.

Main Task

Part 2: Introduce Your Partner

Join another pair to make a group of four.

Tell your new group about the person you just met.

"This is Ali. He has a new laptop on his desk. He also has a photo of his family. This is his sister. Her name is Noura."

Let's Reflect!
