Meeting New People

Grounding Activity

Take a deep breath in.

As you breathe out, imagine hearing the gentle waves.

Take another breath in.

As you breathe out, feel the warmth of the sun.

Arrive in the classroom, ready to begin.

Today's Aims

By the end of this lesson, you will...

  • Use common phrases for greetings and introductions.
  • Correctly use I am and You are.
  • Confidently introduce yourself to a new colleague at a work event.
Pre-task A · 5 minutes

Warm-up: First Impressions

Look at the pictures. What are they doing? What words do they say?

Pre-task B · Restrictive Practice · 7 minutes

Analysing a Conversation

Listen to a conversation between two new colleagues. Then, complete the table.

Transcript

Aliyah: Hello!

Aisha: Hi! What's your name?

Aliyah: My name's Aliyah. And you?

Aisha: I'm Aisha.

Aliyah: Nice to meet you, Aisha.

Aisha: Nice to meet you too, Aliyah.

Tap a token, then tap the matching space in the table.

Question Answer
How many people are speaking?
What are their names?
What question does Aisha ask?
What phrase shows they are happy to meet?
Pre-task B · Semi-controlled Practice · 7 minutes

Rebuilding the Conversation

Rebuild the conversation between Yousef and Sara. Tap a token, then tap the matching blank.

Yousef: Hi! name's Yousef. What's name?

Sara: Hello, Yousef. Sara. Nice to you.

Yousef: Nice to meet , Sara.

Pre-task B · Freer Practice · 6 minutes

Practice the Dialogue

  • With a partner, practice the conversation from the last slide.
  • Student A is Yousef. Student B is Sara. Read the dialogue, then swap roles.
  • Practice again using your own names!
Pre-task C · Restrictive Practice · 7 minutes

Grammar Focus: Key Words

Choose the correct word to complete each sentence.

1. Hello, ______ name is Ali.

2. What's ______ name?

3. Hi, ______ Aisha.

Pre-task C · Semi-controlled Practice · 7 minutes

Pronunciation: Sentence Stress

Listen to your teacher and repeat. The stressed word is the most important information.

  • What's your name?
  • My name's Omar.
  • Nice to meet you.
Pre-task C · Freer Practice · 6 minutes

Quick Questions

Work with a partner. Ask and answer the questions. Use sentence stress on the key words.

  1. Student A: What's your name?
  2. Student B: My name's ______. What's your name?
  3. Student A: I'm ______. Nice to meet you.
  4. Student B: Nice to meet you too.
Pre-task D · Restrictive Practice · 7 minutes

Sentence Building

Put the words in the right order to make a sentence.

  • is / My / Layla / name

  • name / your / What's

  • you / meet / Nice / to

Pre-task D · Semi-controlled Practice · 7 minutes

Imagine You Are...

Imagine you are the person in the picture. Your partner will ask, "What's your name?" Answer them.

Pre-task D · Freer Practice · 6 minutes

Final Rehearsal

Stand up and find a partner. Introduce yourself using your real name. This is your last practice before the main task!

"Hello, my name is..." "What's your name?" "Nice to meet you."
Task

Welcome Event Introductions

Task Preparation

Prepare for the Welcome Event

You are a new employee at a major company in Palestine, like the mobile network Jawwal or the Bank of Palestine.

Today is your first day, and the company is hosting a welcome coffee event for all new staff.

Your goal is to meet at least one new colleague. You have 2 minutes to prepare what you will say.

Task

Welcome to the Team!

Student A · New Employee

You are new to the company. You are a little nervous but also excited. Find someone and introduce yourself.

Student B · Existing Employee

You have worked at the company for two years. Your manager asked you to welcome the new employees. Find someone who looks new and say hello.

Reporting

Introductions at the Team Welcome Meeting

Now that you are back in the main room, we will do an "Introduction Chain." Introduce the colleague you met in the breakout room.

Use the phrase: "This is [classmate's name]."
