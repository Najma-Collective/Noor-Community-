A1 English Lesson

Introductions and Goodbyes

Making a connection from start to finish.

Today's Goals

Combine Language

Use jobs and farewells in one conversation.

Ask Questions

Use Present Simple to ask for info before leaving.

Say Goodbye

Use the right leave-taking expressions.

Role-Play

Practice a full, simple social interaction.

Let's Talk!

A good conversation starts warm.

1. Is it easy or difficult to start a conversation with someone new?

2. What is a polite way to end a conversation?

3. What is more important: a good "hello" or a good "goodbye"?

Activity 1

Combining Jobs & Goodbyes

Let's practice putting the pieces together in a short, logical sequence.

The aim is to use vocabulary in a meaningful way.

A: Hello! I'm a teacher. What do you do?

B: I'm an engineer.

A: Nice to meet you. Well, I have to go. Have a good evening!

Activity 1: Your Turn!

In breakout rooms, practice a short conversation with your partner.

Your conversation must include:

  • At least one job word (student, teacher, etc.).
  • At least one farewell phrase (goodbye, see you later).

Activity 2

Asking Questions Before You Go

Asking one last question before leaving makes the transition feel natural and friendly.

Example Transition

"It was nice talking to you. Before I go, where do you live? Maybe we are neighbours."

Coffee Shop Scenario

You are at a coffee shop talking to a new person. You need to leave.

Your task:

  • Ask one more question (What do you do? or Where do you live?).
  • Then, politely say goodbye.

Activity 3

Using the Right Farewell

Choosing the right phrase depends on the context.

On a Friday at 5 PM...

You can say: "Have a good weekend!"

To a colleague you'll see tomorrow...

You can say: "See you tomorrow!"

Bus Stop Role-Play

Activity 3

Your Roles (in pairs):

Student A: You are waiting for a bus.

Student B: You arrive at the bus stop.

Your Task:

Start a short conversation, ask what they do, and say goodbye when one person's bus arrives!

Activity 4: Language Exchange Mixer!

Time to put it all together. The goal is speed and confidence.

Your conversations will be short, friendly, and complete.

Mixer Instructions

In small groups of 3-4:

  • Have a short conversation with every person in your group.
  • Ask "What do you do?"
  • Use a different farewell phrase each time, if you can.

The Main Task

Role-Play: First Day at a New Job

This is a very realistic context. Give it your best shot!

Scenario: The Office Kitchen

Student A: New Employee

It's your first day. Go to the kitchen for a coffee and introduce yourself to a colleague.

Student B: Current Employee

You have worked here for a long time. You see a new person in the kitchen. Be friendly!

Follow-Up Task

1. Report Back

Tell your new group about the colleague you just met.

"I met [Name]. She is an engineer. I said 'see you later'."

2. Discuss

What is good advice for a new employee on their first day? How can they make a good impression?
