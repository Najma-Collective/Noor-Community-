A1 English Lesson

All About You

Practice introducing yourself and getting to know others.

Today's Goals

Ask for Details

Practice asking for name, nationality, and email.

Talk about Work

Practice asking about jobs and where people live.

Combine Language

Use all your new questions in one conversation.

Role-Play

Practice a full conversation in a realistic scenario.

Let's Talk!

When you meet someone new...

1. What is the first question you ask?

2. What is the next question?

Activity 1

Guess Who!

Work with a partner. One person thinks of a new identity. The other person asks questions to discover the secret identity!

Then, swap roles!

Questions to Ask:

  • What's your name?
  • What's your job?
  • What's your nationality?
  • What's your email?

Find the Profession!

Activity 2

Your Task (in small groups):

In your group, one person is a doctor, one is an engineer, and one is a student.

Ask questions with "What's...?" and "Are you...?" to find out who is who.

Activity 3

Mini-Survey

Practice asking more questions with a partner. Use the list below.

Survey Questions:

  • Do you live in [City Name]?
  • Do you work in [City Name]?
  • Do you have a phone number?
  • Do you have an email?

Activity 4: Making New Friends

Imagine your friend is new at university and wants to make friends.

In your groups, decide on the TOP 3 most important questions to ask a new person.

The Main Task

Role-Play: Joining a University Club

Time to use all your language in a realistic conversation.

Scenario: The English Conversation Club

Student A: Club Manager

Ask questions to register a new member. You need their name, nationality, email, where they live, and what they do.

Student B: New Member

You want to join the club. Answer the manager's questions.

Follow-Up Task

1. Report Back

Join another pair and introduce the new member you just registered.

"This is my new member. His name is Ali. He is from Palestine. He is a student..."

2. Discuss

After you introduce your members, discuss: What makes a good conversation club?

Let's Reflect!

Think about your own learning today.

Which question is easy for you now?

What was one new word you used today?

Was the final task easy or difficult for you?

Great work today!

See you next time!
