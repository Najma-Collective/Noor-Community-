Conversation Synthesis

Getting to Know You

Fast introductions and confident follow-up questions

  • Level: A1 (Beginner)
  • Duration: 60 minutes
  • Audience: Palestinian Adult Learners (Online)
Main Objective

By the end of this lesson, students will...

Synthesise language for introductions and personal details so they can meet a new person and exchange contact information in social or professional contexts.

Lead-in · 5 mins

Warm-up questions

Procedure

  1. Ask students starter questions to activate prior knowledge.

Teacher's Script & Key Language

  • “When you meet a new person, what is the first thing you say?”
  • “What is the first question you ask a new person?”
  • “Imagine you are at a new class or event. What information do you sometimes need to give people?”

Teacher's Tip

Encourage relaxed, personal responses. Keep language simple, use gestures, and avoid heavy correction so everyone feels ready to participate.

Activity 1 · 20 mins

Combining lessons 1 & 2

Focus: Combining vocabulary from both lessons.

Procedure

  1. Model a short conversation with a student.
  2. Remind learners: “First, we say hello (Lesson 1). Then, we ask for information (Lesson 2).”
  3. Place students in pairs.
  4. Partners greet each other, share names, and ask for nationality and phone number.

Teacher's Script & Key Language

  • Say: “Hello! My name is [Teacher's Name]. Nice to meet you.”
    Elicit/Expect: “Hi! I'm [Student's Name]. Nice to meet you too.”
  • Say: “Great! What's your email, [Student's Name]?”
    Elicit/Expect: “My email is…”

Teacher's Tip

Monitor breakout rooms for common errors but let conversations flow naturally. The aim is meaningful practice of the complete exchange.

Activity 2 · 20 mins

Subject pronouns + to be

Focus: Using “Subject Pronouns + to be” for personal details.

Procedure

  1. Review the grammar on screen: “I am…”, “My … is…”.
  2. Give a model sentence using new vocabulary.
  3. Put students into small groups.
  4. Students share three facts about themselves using the target structures.

Teacher's Script & Key Language

  • Say: “I am from Palestine. My phone number is 059… My email is teacher@email.com.”
    Elicit/Expect: Students share similar statements.
  • Model: “My name is Fatima. I am from Hebron. My address is An-Naser Street.”

Teacher's Tip

Focus on fluency. Let learners invent details if it helps them speak. The grammar supports communication—it isn't the main event.

Activity 3 · 20 mins

Wh- questions in introductions

Focus: Using “Wh- questions with to be” to welcome someone new.

Procedure

  1. Review example questions with the whole class.
  2. Demonstrate how the questions sound in a friendly conversation.
  3. Pair students for role-play.
  4. Role-play: one student is new at a language centre; the other introduces themselves and gathers key details, then swap.

Teacher's Script & Key Language

  • Ask: “How do I ask for your name?”
    Elicit/Expect: “What’s your name?”
  • Ask: “How do I ask for your country?”
    Elicit/Expect: “Where are you from?”
  • Model: “Hello, I'm Ahmed. It's nice to meet you. What's your name?”

Teacher's Tip

Encourage learners to include “Nice to meet you” for natural tone. This keeps the practice social rather than transactional.

Activity 4 · 20 mins

Community role-play

Focus: Integrated practice of all target language.

Procedure

  1. Present the scenario: meeting new friends at a community event.
  2. Gather initial ideas from the whole class.
  3. Form small groups.
  4. Group members introduce themselves and collect names and phone numbers for a “new friends” list.

Teacher's Tip

This realistic task unites all language from the lesson. Monitor and support with vocabulary or prompts as needed.

Main Task 1 · 15 mins

Tour company sign-up

Focus: Final role-play collecting personal details.

Procedure

  1. Pair students as roles A and B.
  2. Explain: Student A works for a tour company; Student B is a visitor.
  3. Role-play the sign-up conversation, including introductions and exchanging name, nationality, and email.

Teacher's Tip

Give preparation time and let the dialogue develop naturally. This combines professional context with personal information gathering.

Main Task 2 · 15 mins

Group reporting

Focus: Reporting and comprehension.

Procedure

  1. Combine two pairs to form groups of four.
  2. Each student reports the key details they gathered from their partner.
  3. Groups discuss: “How many different nationalities are in your list?”

Teacher's Script & Key Language

  • Guide: “Student A (tour guide) reports: ‘I met [Student B's name]. He/She is from [Country]. His/Her email is…’”
  • Guide: “Student B (visitor) reports: ‘I met [Student A's name]. He/She works for a tour company.’”

Teacher's Tip

Reporting keeps students accountable for listening and reuse. The final question checks understanding and encourages collaboration.

Reflection · 5 mins

Check-in & celebrate

Prompts

  • “Today, was it easy or difficult to ask for information?”
  • “What new words do you remember? (name, email, etc.)”
  • “Can you say ‘hello’ and ask ‘What’s your name?’ now? (Thumbs up/down.)”

Teacher's Tip

Keep the wrap-up short and positive. Praise effort and use visual checks like thumbs up/down to make it accessible for all learners.
