Speaking Lesson

Joining the Club

Asking for and Giving Personal Information

Learning Objectives

  • Ask for and share basic personal details politely.
  • Use Wh- questions and statements with the verb "to be".
  • Use falling intonation in Wh- questions.

Lesson Flow

  1. Grounding & schema activation
  2. Model dialogue and controlled practice
  3. Grammar and pronunciation focus
  4. Role-play task and reflection

Step 2 · Grounding

Let's Begin Together

  • Take a deep breath in.

Step 3

Today's Aims

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Ask for personal details like name, nationality, and address.
  • Give your personal details clearly.
  • Use the verb to be (am, is, are) correctly.
  • Use falling intonation for Wh- questions (e.g., What's your name? ↘).
Step 4 · Pre-task A · 5 minutes

Museum Membership

Look at the image. Imagine you are at a museum in Palestine. Work with a partner and discuss these questions:

  1. What are these cards?
  2. Why do people get them?
  3. What information is on them?
Step 5 · Pre-task B · 7 minutes

At the Reception Desk

Listen & Read

Receptionist: Hello. Can I help you?

Visitor: Yes, I'd like to sign up for a membership card.

Receptionist: Of course. What's your name?

Visitor: My name is Omar Hassan.

Receptionist: And where are you from?

Visitor: I'm from Egypt.

Receptionist: Great. And what's your address?

Visitor: My address is 15 Al-Quds Street, Ramallah.

Match the Questions and Answers

Listen to the conversation. Then match the receptionist's questions to the visitor's answers.

Receptionist's Questions

1. What's your name?
2. Where are you from?
3. What's your address?
Step 6 · Pre-task B · 7 minutes

Checking Understanding

Read the dialogue again. Answer the questions with your partner.

  1. What does the visitor want?
  2. What is the receptionist's first question?
  3. Is Omar from Morocco?
  4. What three pieces of information does Omar give?
Step 7 · Pre-task B · 6 minutes

Practice with a New Identity

Imagine you are this person. Your partner is the receptionist. Practice the conversation from the model.

  • Use the same question order.
  • Give complete answers using the details on the card.
  • Switch roles and repeat.

Step 14

Task

Step 15 · Task Preparation

Get Ready to Role-Play

You are at the grand opening of The Palestinian Heritage Museum in Ramallah. You want to become a member to get discounts and special access to exhibits. You will go to the reception desk to sign up.

  • Decide who is Role A (New Member) and Role B (Receptionist).
  • Role A: Plan a fictional name, nationality, and address.
  • Role B: Plan how you will greet the member.
  • You have 2 minutes to prepare.
Step 16 · Task

Role-Play: Signing Up

Role A · New Member

  • You are at The Palestinian Heritage Museum.
  • You want to sign up for a membership card.
  • Give the receptionist your details.
  • Be polite and ask for the receptionist's name at the end.

Role B · Receptionist

  • You work at The Palestinian Heritage Museum.
  • Welcome the visitor.
  • Ask for their name, nationality, and address.
  • When they ask, tell them your name is Sam.

Perform the conversation for 4-5 minutes.

Step 17 · Reporting

Briefing the Manager

After your role-play, your manager (the teacher) will ask a few receptionists:

  • Did the new member sign up successfully?
  • What were their details?

Be ready to report the name and nationality of the new member you spoke to.

Step 18 · Homework

Homework Practice

Activity 2 · Complete the Dialogue

Fill the gaps with: am, is, are, My, your.

B: (2)______ name (3)______ Paolo.

A: And where (4)______ you from, Paolo?
