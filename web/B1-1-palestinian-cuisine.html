Traditional Palestinian Musakhan

The National Dish of Palestine

Ingredients

  • Much olive oil (about 1 cup) - the heart of the dish
  • A few large onions (4-5), sliced thin
  • Some sumac (3-4 tablespoons)
  • A little salt and pepper
  • Several pieces of chicken (1 whole chicken, cut up)
  • A lot of taboon bread (or substitute with pita)
  • A few pine nuts for garnish (optional)
  • Many memories and lots of love

Instructions

You need plenty of patience for this dish. First, caramelize the onions in much olive oil. Add several spoonfuls of sumac. The color should be deep purple. Cook the chicken with some spices until tender. Layer the bread with onions and chicken. Don't use too much bread - you want enough space for the toppings.

Pro tip: You can never have too many onions in Musakhan. In Palestine, we say "al-basal rab il-akl" - onions are the lord of all food!

Concept Checking Questions (CCQs)

Q1: Can we say "many olive oil"? Why or why not?

Answer: No, because "oil" is uncountable. We say "much oil" or "a lot of oil"

Q2: What's the difference between "a few" and "a little"?

Answer: "A few" is for countable (a few onions), "a little" is for uncountable (a little salt)

Q3: Can we use "some" with both countable and uncountable nouns?

Answer: Yes! (some onions, some oil)

Part 5: Variant Text - Restaurant Dialogue

Read this conversation at a Palestinian restaurant in Ramallah.

Waiter: Good evening! Welcome to Beit Hanina Restaurant. What would you like to order?
Customer: Hi! Do you have any Musakhan today?
Waiter: Yes, we have plenty of Musakhan. We also have some Maqluba left, but not much.
Customer: Great! I'll have the Musakhan. How many pieces of chicken come with it?
Waiter: You get several pieces - usually four or five. Would you like a little extra sumac on top?
Customer: Yes please! And can I have a few extra pieces of bread?
Waiter: Of course. We never say no to more bread. Would you like any drinks? We have lots of fresh lemon juice today.
Customer: Perfect! A glass of lemon juice, please. Not too much ice.
Waiter: Wonderful choice! Your order will be ready in a few minutes.
