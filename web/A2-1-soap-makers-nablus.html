Part 1: Reading - The Factory Infographic

Read about the traditional soap-making process in Nablus.

Nabulsi Soap: The Green Gold

Nablus soap is older than most soaps in the world. It has been made since the 10th century. The ingredients are simple: pure olive oil, water, and soda.

The Process vs. Modern Soap

Cooking: The mixture is cooked in large vats. This is the hottest part of the process.

Drying: The liquid soap is spread on the stone floor. It is harder than liquid soap, but it takes time to dry.

The Tanana: Workers stack the soap in towers called Tanana. These towers are the highest structures in the factory. They allow air to circulate.

Packaging: The soap is wrapped by hand. This is slower than machine packing, but it protects the soap better.

Nabulsi soap is the best for sensitive skin because it has no chemicals. It is slightly more expensive than supermarket soap, but it lasts longer.

CCQs - Concept Checking Questions

Q1: "Nabulsi soap is older than most soaps." Are we comparing to one soap or many?

Answer: Many soaps

Q2: "The Tanana are the highest structures." Is there any structure higher in the factory?

Answer: No

Q3: Why do we say "more expensive" and not "expensiver"?

Answer: Because it's a long word (3 syllables)
