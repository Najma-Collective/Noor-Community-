Lesson Aims

Today's Aims

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Ask and answer questions about past activities with colleagues.
  • Use past time expressions like yesterday, last week, and on Saturday.
  • Pronounce regular past tense verbs with -ed endings correctly.

Pre-task B · Semi-controlled Practice · 7 minutes

Dialogue Deconstruction

Model Dialogue

Maria: Hi Ben. How was your weekend?

Ben: It was good, thanks.

Maria: What did you do?

Ben: I visited the new park on Saturday.

Maria: Oh, nice! Did you go with friends?

Ben: Yes, I did. I went with two friends. We walked for hours!

Comprehension

Type the missing words to check your understanding of the dialogue.

  1. Ben went to the park on .
  2. At the park, he .
  3. He went with .

Highlight the different question forms in the dialogue. Which ones ask for information and which ones confirm details?

Pre-task C · Restrictive Practice · 7 minutes

Asking the Right Questions

Choose the question that matches the answer. Focus on the difference between information questions and confirmation questions.

1. Answer: I went to the beach. (Asks for information)

2. Answer: Yes, I did. (Confirms information)

3. Answer: On Saturday. (Asks for information)

4. Answer: No, I didn't. I stayed home. (Confirms information)

5. Answer: I saw my friends. (Asks for information)

Pre-task C · Semi-controlled Practice · 7 minutes

Question Formation Practice

You want to ask your colleague about their weekend. Turn each prompt into a full question. Capitalise the first word and include the question mark.

  1. (you / watch / TV / yesterday?)
  2. (what / you / do / last night?)
  3. (you / visit / family / on Sunday?)
  4. (where / you / go / on Saturday?)
  5. (she / work / last week?)

Compare with a partner. Which questions ask for new information and which confirm something you think is true?

Main Task

Task

Task Preparation

Prepare for Your Chat

Scenario: It's Monday morning. You meet your colleague in the office kitchen. It's the first real chat of the week. Your colleague was off work on Friday.

Your Goal:

  • Ask your colleague about their long weekend.
  • Answer their questions about your regular weekend.

Preparation (3 minutes): Read your role card. Plan your questions and answers.

Task

Role-Play: Monday Morning Catch-up

Pair up, choose a role card, and create a warm Monday catch-up that sounds natural and curious.

Role Card A

  • You had a normal weekend (Saturday & Sunday).
  • Saturday: You stayed home and watched movies.
  • Sunday: You visited your parents.
  • Your focus: ask about your colleague's long weekend.

Role Card B

  • You enjoyed a long weekend (off on Friday).
  • Friday: You went to the beach.
  • Saturday: You saw your friends.
  • Your focus: answer and ask about their regular weekend.

Success Checklist

  • Ask at least two Wh- questions.
  • Include at least one Yes/No question.
  • Use three different past tense verbs accurately.
Reporting

Team Meeting Update

Imagine it's your team's morning meeting. The manager asks, "Did everyone have a good weekend?" Share one interesting thing your partner did.

Example: "It sounds like Anna had a great weekend. She visited the new park on Saturday."
