A2 English Lesson

Telling Your Story

Connecting experiences from work, study, and travel.

Today's Goals

Connect Experiences

Talk about past events and how they relate to your present life.

Tell a Story

Share a short story about your career, education, or a memorable trip.

Practice Tenses

Use the Past Simple for finished actions and the Present Simple for now.

Let's Talk!

1. Do you ever tell stories at work or in class?

2. Have you ever taken a trip for work or for your studies? Where did you go?

3. Think about a story a friend told you recently. What was it about?

Activity 1

Connecting Words

Let's combine vocabulary from different topics.

For example:

Work + Event:

For my job, I am responsible for organizing the annual company celebration.

Study + Travel:

After I graduated, I took a trip to visit a famous historic building.

Activity 1: Your Turn!

With a partner, create a short dialogue about a job in the tourism industry.

Your dialogue must include:

  • Two words about work/study (e.g., graduated, responsible for).
  • Two words about travel/events (e.g., festival, visit, museum).

Activity 2

Your Career Story

Use time connectors to tell a story in the correct order.

Example Story

First, I studied English at university.

Then, I worked in a small school.

After that, I moved to a different city.

Finally, I started teaching online.

Tell Your Story

Activity 2: Your Turn!

In your group, take turns telling the story of your academic or professional journey.

Remember to:

Use at least three time connectors (First, Then, Finally, etc.).

Use the Past Simple for finished actions.

Activity 3

Past & Present

Let's connect a past experience to how things are now.

Example:

Past:

Last year, I visited a big food festival. It was amazing. There were so many people.

Present:

Now, I live in a small village, so I don't go to big events.

Then and Now

Activity 3: Your Turn!

With your partner, talk about a memorable trip or event from your past.

  1. Describe the event using the Past Simple.
  2. Explain how your life or feelings are different now using the Present Simple.

Activity 4

A Story for an Interview

Let's practice creating a story for a specific situation.

The Scenario:

Your friend has an important job interview tomorrow.

The interviewer wants to hear a story about a time your friend solved a problem.

What story from their past (a trip, a celebration, a past job) could they tell?

Problem-Solving Story

Activity 4: Your Turn!

In your group, create a short story for your friend to tell at the interview.

Your story must:

1. Use time connectors and the Past Simple to structure the events.

2. End with one sentence in the Present Simple about what they learned.

Example ending: "...Finally, I found my passport. Now, I am always very organised."

Main Task: The Job Interview

Time to put it all together!

Part 1: The Role-Play

In pairs, one is the Interviewer, one is the Applicant for a Tour Guide job.

The Applicant's Task:

  1. Talk about your past education and work (Past Simple).
  2. Talk about your current situation (Present Simple).
  3. Tell a short story about a memorable travel experience or festival you attended (Past Simple + Time Connectors).

Main Task

Part 2: The Hiring Committee

Join another pair to make a group of four.

Report and Decide

The two "interviewers" will report on their candidate to the group. You should summarize their background and retell their story.

As a group, discuss and decide: Who gets the job? Why?

Let's Reflect!

Think about your learning today.

In the job interview, what was easier: talking about your past or your present? Why?

Which time connector (first, then, finally) did you use the most today?

What is one thing you feel more confident about now?

Great work today!

See you next time!
