A2 English Lesson

Storytelling Workshop

Let's talk about our travels and experiences.

Today's Goals

Tell a Story in Order

Use Past Simple and sequencing words like first, then, finally.

Combine Vocabulary

Talk about tourism (museum, park) and daily events (missed the bus).

Ask Good Questions

Use Past Simple questions like 'Did you...?' to learn more.

Let's Talk!

1. When was the last time you visited a different town or city?

2. Do you prefer visiting places like museums, or just walking around and seeing what happens?

3. Did anything funny or unexpected happen on your last trip?

Activity 1

A Tourist's Mishap

Let's combine our vocabulary. We can use tourism words with daily incident words.

For example:

"First, he wanted to visit a historic building..."

"...but he missed the bus."

"Then, he walked to the park and found some money!"

Activity 1: Your Turn!

With your partner, create a short story about a very lucky or very unlucky tourist.

Your story must include:

  • At least two tourism words (museum, park, gallery...)
  • At least two incident words (missed the bus, saw a friend...)

Activity 2

A Memorable Day

To tell a story, we use Past Simple verbs and sequencing words.

"Last month, I visited Nablus.
First, I walked through the old city.
Then, I ate some knafeh.
After that, I visited Jacob's Well.
Finally, I took the bus home."

Your Trip

Activity 2: Your Turn!

In your group, talk about the most memorable day trip you have ever taken.

Tell your group:

1. Where did you go?

2. What did you do?

Use sequencing words to tell the story of your day.

Activity 3

Ask for Details!

A good story is a conversation. Use Past Simple questions to learn more.

For example:

Storyteller: "Yesterday, I went to a new place."

Listener: "Oh, interesting! Where did you go?"

Listener: "What did you see there?"

Listener: "Who did you go with?"

Story Detective

Activity 3: Your Turn!

  1. Student A starts a story with only ONE sentence.
    (e.g., "Last week, I went to the city center.")
  2. Student B asks at least THREE Past Simple questions to get more details.
  3. Swap roles!

Activity 4

The Perfect Day Trip

Read the problem below. In your group, you will help your friend.

The Problem:

Your friend from another country is visiting you for the first time. They only have one day in your city. You need to plan the perfect day for them!

Brainstorm!

Activity 4: Your Turn!

In your group, discuss and plan a one-day itinerary for your friend.

Plan the story of the day:

1. First, where will you go? What will you do?

2. Then, what's the next stop?

3. Finally, how will you end the day?

Be ready to present your plan to the class.

Main Task: The Story Director

Let's put everything together in a longer conversation!

Part 1: The Conversation

In pairs, you will have two roles: the Storyteller and the Director.

The Storyteller

Think of a real trip you took. Start by saying only the first sentence of the story.

e.g., "Last summer, my family and I went to the beach for a day."

The Director

Your job is to uncover the rest of the story. Ask questions using the Past Simple to find out what happened.

e.g., "What did you do first?", "Did you swim?", "Did anything funny happen?"

Main Task

Part 2: Report Back

Join another pair to make a group of four.

Tell your new group about your first partner's story.

"I spoke with Ahmed. He told me about his trip to the beach. First, his family put up an umbrella. Then, he went swimming..."
