Model Dialogue: A Coffee Break in the Office

Model

Setting: Laila and Kareem are new colleagues meeting in their office kitchen in Ramallah.

Laila: Hello. I'm Laila.
Kareem: Hi Laila! I'm Kareem. Nice to meet you.
Laila: Nice to meet you too. So, Kareem, what do you do?
Kareem: I work in the office here. I answer emails and I answer the phone. What about you?
Laila: I am a teacher.
Kareem: Oh, that's great. Do you want coffee?
Laila: Yes, please. Thank you!

            **Setting:** Laila and Kareem are new colleagues meeting in their office kitchen in Ramallah.

        Read the dialogue. Choose the correct answer for each question.
        [Activity_Module: Multiple_Choice]
        
            1. Where are Laila and Kareem talking?
            
                a) In a school
                b) In an office
                c) In a café
            
            b
            
                Not quite. Laila is a teacher, but the dialogue says Kareem works "in the office here." This is the location of the conversation.
                Good thinking, they talk about coffee, but the setting says they are in their "office kitchen".
            
        
        
            2. What is Kareem's job?
            
                a) He is a teacher.
                b) He works in an office.
                c) He makes coffee.
            
            b
            
                This is Laila's job. In the dialogue, she says, "I am a teacher."
                He offers to make coffee, but this is not his main job. He says, "I work in the office here."
            
        
        
            3. What are two of Kareem's tasks?
            
                a) Meet Laila and drink coffee.
                b) Answer emails and answer the phone.
                c) Be a teacher and work in an office.
            
            b
            
                These are things that happen in the conversation, but they are not his regular job tasks. He says, "I answer emails and I answer the phone."
                Laila is a teacher, and Kareem works in an office. These are their jobs, not two tasks Kareem does. Look for the two actions he mentions together.
