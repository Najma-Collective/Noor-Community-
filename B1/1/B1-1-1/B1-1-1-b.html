B1 English Lesson

Making Professional Connections

From simple introductions to meaningful conversations.

Today's Goals

Introduce Yourself

Clearly state your job and what you are responsible for.

Ask Good Questions

Learn about others' professional background and experience.

Find Collaborations

Use your conversation skills to discover ways to work together.

Let's Talk!

1. After you introduce your name and job, what is the very next thing you say or ask?

2. Why is it important to ask good questions at a networking event?

3. What's the difference between a simple introduction and making a real professional connection?

Activity 1

Connecting Ideas

Let's combine vocabulary about job roles with words for professional background.

For example:

To be a successful project manager, you need a strong background in communication.

A good developer often has expertise in a specific coding language.

Activity 1: Your Ideal Job

With a partner, create a short job description for your ideal job.

Your description should include:

  • The role and responsibilities (e.g., in charge of, handle...).
  • The required qualifications (e.g., experience, expertise, background).

Activity 2

Finding a Partner

Use the Present Simple to describe what you do and what you need.

Example

"My team develops mobile apps. We often look for partners who specialize in marketing."

"I handle the finances, so I connect with people who have expertise in investment."

What do you need?

Activity 2: Your Turn!

In your group, discuss this question:

Discussion Prompt

Think about your company or your field of study. What kind of work do you do?

Based on that, what kind of professional partner do you often look for?

Activity 3

Asking Good Questions

Use Wh- questions to learn more after an introduction.

Example:

A: Hi, I'm a marketing specialist.

B: That's interesting. What exactly do you do day-to-day?

A: I handle social media.

B: Great. Where did you get your experience in that field?

The Curious Colleague

Activity 3: Your Turn!

Practice with a partner in a mini role-play.

  1. Student A: Introduce your job title and one responsibility.
  2. Student B: Ask at least four follow-up Wh- questions to learn more.
  3. Switch roles!

Activity 4

The 3-Minute Strategy

Let's prepare for a very short, important conversation.

The Scenario:

You are at a networking event and you meet someone very important, but you only have three minutes to talk.

Your Goal: Make a great impression and decide if you should connect later.

Create Your Strategy

Activity 4: Your Turn!

In your group, create a "3-Minute Networking Strategy."

Your strategy must include:

1. Introduction (2-3 sentences): What will you say to introduce yourself and your work?

2. Essential Questions (2-3 questions): What will you ask to learn about them?

Main Task: The Networking Event

Time to find a new partner for a project!

Part 1: The Role-Play

You are at an online conference for Palestinian professionals. Your company has a problem, and you need to find someone who can help.

Your Task:

  1. Introduce yourself (your role, responsibilities, background).
  2. Ask your partner questions to discover their expertise.
  3. Explain your problem and see if they can help.
  4. Decide if you should schedule a follow-up call.

Main Task

Part 2: Report & Discuss

Join another pair to make a group of four.

Report and Decide

Summarize your partner's professional role and background to the group. Explain why you decided to schedule (or not schedule) a follow-up call.

As a group, discuss: Which of the two potential collaborations sounds more promising?
