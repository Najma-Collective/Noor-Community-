Building Professional Networks

Introducing Yourself and Your Professional Role

Grounding Activity

Arrival Breathwork Ritual

Breathe In Possibility Take a deep breath. Imagine you are about to enter a room full of new opportunities. Exhale any nervousness. Let your posture, breath, and focus prepare you to make a remarkable first impression.

Today's Aims

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to...

  • Confidently introduce your professional role in a networking context.
  • Accurately describe your responsibilities using the Present Simple.
  • Ask and answer common networking questions.
  • Pronounce job titles clearly and correctly.

Pre-task B · Semi-controlled Practice · 7 minutes

The Introduction Models

Model 1: Project-Focused

Aysha: Hi, I'm Aysha. Great to connect. What do you do?

Brian: Hi Aysha, I'm Brian. I'm a Project Manager at "Connective Core" in Dublin. I'm in charge of our new mobile platform launch. And you?

Aysha: I'm a Developer for "Gaza Sky Geeks" in Ramallah. My team develops educational software for young people.

Model 2: Responsibility-Focused

Fatima: Hello, my name is Fatima. It’s a pleasure to meet you.

Liam: You too, Fatima. I'm Liam. What brings you to the summit?

Fatima: I'm a Data Analyst with a fintech company in Ramallah. I handle all our market performance metrics. I'm here to learn about new data visualization tools. How about you?

Liam: I'm a Marketing Specialist. I am responsible for user acquisition strategy at my firm in Dublin.

Instructions: Read both introductions. What is different about their focus? Be ready to share one observation.

Task · 5 minutes per round

Speed Networking Session

Instructions:

  • You will be moved into a series of breakout rooms for 5 minutes each.
  • In each room, introduce yourself to your partner(s) and learn about their roles and responsibilities.
  • Share something interesting about your work and note who you want to reconnect with.
  • Remember your goal: find three people you want to connect with again.

Role Card A

Company: "Jaffa Creative Labs" (Ramallah)

Title: UX/UI Designer

Details: You create user-friendly interfaces for a new language-learning app. You are looking for developers who have experience with gamification.

Role Card B

Company: "Emerald Innovations" (Dublin)

Title: Marketing Specialist

Details: You handle the European marketing strategy for your company's software. You are looking for project managers who understand market entry in the MENA region.

Reporting

Post-Event Debrief

Imagine you are debriefing with the event organizer. Be prepared to share with the group:

  • The name and role of the most interesting person you met.
  • Why you believe they are a valuable professional connection for the future.

Use Present Simple verbs to summarize your partner's responsibilities clearly.

Practice Activities

Synchronous Skill Reinforcement

Immediate, controlled practice to consolidate today's grammar and vocabulary targets.

Activity 1: Grammar & Vocabulary Quickfire

Type: Multiple-choice grammar

Instructions: Choose the correct word or phrase to complete each sentence.

  1. A project manager often ______ with many different teams.
    a) liaise b) liaises
  2. In my current role, I ______ the entire marketing budget.
    a) handle b) handles
  3. Our developers in Ramallah ______ on the back-end systems.
    a) focus b) focuses
  4. She is ______ for the user interface design.
    a) in charge b) responsible
  5. The data analyst ______ detailed reports for the leadership team.
    a) prepare b) prepares

Rubric: 1 point per correct response (5 points total).
