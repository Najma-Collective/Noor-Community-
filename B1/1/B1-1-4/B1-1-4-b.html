B1 English Lesson

Making Meaningful Connections

From Small Talk to Collaboration.

Today's Goals

Combine Language

Use phrases for showing interest with vocabulary about professional backgrounds.

Ask About Experience

Use Present Perfect ('Have you ever...?') for general experience.

Ask For Details

Use Past Simple ('What did you...?') for specific information.

Find Collaboration

Role-play a networking conversation to find a real collaboration idea.

Let's Talk!

The Art of Conversation

1. How do you move a conversation from a simple "hello" to a real discussion?

2. What's the difference between a "common interest" and a "collaboration opportunity"?

3. Have you ever found an opportunity (for work, study, etc.) just by talking to someone?

Activity 1

Show You're Listening

Let's connect phrases that show interest with words about professional backgrounds.

For example:

"You mentioned your field is in software development."

"That's interesting! Tell me more about your expertise in mobile applications."

Activity 1: Your Turn!

In pairs, have a conversation.

Your Task:

  • Partner A: Describe a professional project.
  • Partner B: Use "showing interest" phrases to discover your partner's field, background, and expertise.
  • Switch roles after 6 minutes!

Activity 2

The Big Picture

To learn about someone's general life experiences, use the Present Perfect.

Ask questions like:

"Have you ever managed a team?"

"Have you worked with international clients?"

Share Your Experiences

Activity 2: Your Turn!

In small groups, discuss these questions:

  • Talk about a professional skill you have learned that could be useful.
  • Describe a professional success you have had.
  • Have you ever faced a major challenge at work or university?

Activity 3

Asking for Details

To ask about specific events in the past, use the Past Simple.

For example:

"I see you attended the tech conference last week."

"What sessions did you attend?"

"Who did you find most interesting?"

Get the Details!

Activity 3

Activity 3: Your Turn!

In pairs, role-play this scenario.

Your Task:

  1. Partner A: You just attended an interesting professional event.
  2. Partner B: Ask specific Past Simple questions to learn about the event.
  3. Swap roles and practice again!

Activity 4: The 5-Minute Challenge

You meet a potential collaborator, but you only have five minutes.

What are the best questions to ask?

Activity 4: Your Turn!

Your Top 5 Questions

In your groups, brainstorm and create a list of "The Top 5 Questions for a 5-Minute Networking Conversation."

  • Use both Present Perfect (general) and Past Simple (specific).
  • Be ready to share your list and explain your choices!

The Main Task

Role-Play: The Entrepreneur Conference

Now, let's put everything together in a realistic conversation.

Scenario: A Conference for Palestinian Entrepreneurs

In pairs, have a conversation. Follow these steps:

1. Connect

Find a common professional interest with your partner.

2. Explore

Ask questions (Past Simple & Present Perfect) to see if there is potential for collaboration.

3. Decide

Agree on a potential collaboration idea you could work on together.

Follow-Up Task

Report Back & Discuss

Join another pair to make a group of four.

1. Report on your conversation.

"I spoke with [Name]. We have both worked on educational projects. Last year, she designed an online course..."

2. Discuss which collaboration sounds more promising and why.
