Use small talk & formal phrases together.

Use Past Simple and Present Perfect to share experiences.

Let's Talk!

Your Networking Experiences

1. Think about the last new person you met professionally. Did you contact them again? Why or why not?

2. Why is it important to "follow up" after meeting someone? What can you achieve?

3. How is a follow-up call different from the first conversation?

Activity 1

Mixing Our Language

Let's practice combining phrases for small talk and follow-up in a phone call.

For example:

To start, you can say, "Hello, it was a pleasure to meet you."

To remind them, you can say, "I thought our conversation was really interesting!"

Activity 1: Your Turn!

In pairs, create and practice a short dialogue for the start of a follow-up phone call.

Your dialogue must include:

  • At least one formal phrase for referring to a past meeting.
  • At least one small talk phrase to show interest.

Activity 2

Sharing Your Experience

In a follow-up call, you can share more about your background to build a connection.

You can use the Present Perfect:

"That's interesting, I've worked on three projects like that before."

"Have you ever considered using this software?"

Your Dream Job

Activity 2: Your Turn!

Imagine you're calling a contact who works in your dream job. In your groups, discuss:

  • What relevant experiences have you had?
  • What useful skills have you learned?
  • Have you ever failed at something and learned from it?

Activity 3

Planning in the First Chat

You can use the first conversation to plan for the next one.

Talk about the event (Past Simple)

"I really enjoyed the presentation this morning."

"I thought your comments were very interesting."

Make a future plan

"Perhaps we could continue this conversation later?"

"I'll send you an email with my details."

First Impressions

Activity 3

Activity 3: Your Turn!

In pairs, role-play meeting for the first time at a conference.

Your Task:

  1. Introduce yourselves.
  2. Use the Past Simple to talk about a session you attended.
  3. Use a future form to make a plan to connect again later.

Activity 4: Giving Advice

Let's use all our language to solve a problem.

Scenario: Your friend met an important person last week but is nervous about calling them. What should they do?

Activity 4: Your Turn!

Create a Plan

In your groups, brainstorm a step-by-step plan for your nervous friend.

  • What should they say first? (e.g., "They should say when they met...")
  • What questions can they ask? (e.g., "...then ask, 'Have you ever...?''')
  • How should they end the call? (e.g., "Maybe they could suggest a meeting.")

The Main Task

Role-Play: The Follow-Up Phone Call

Time to put it all together. Let's practice a full, realistic conversation!

Scenario: The Follow-Up Call

In pairs, role-play the phone call based on your meeting last week.

Student A: The Caller

Your goal is to re-establish the connection and propose a next step. Use the Past Simple ('We met last week...') and future forms ('Perhaps we could...').

Student B: The Receiver

Your goal is to be friendly and professional. Use small talk phrases ('That's interesting!') and the Present Perfect to share experiences ('I've worked on...').

Follow-Up Task

Report Back

Join another pair to make a group of four.

Tell your new group about your call.

"I called [Name]. I reminded her that we met at the conference. She told me she has worked with several NGOs. I suggested we could have a Zoom call next week."
