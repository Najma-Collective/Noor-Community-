Activity 1

Two truths and a lie — advanced edition

Craft three statements about yourself. Make the lie sound convincing so your classmates have to listen carefully and justify their guess.

  • Write one surprising detail, one achievement, and one decoy.
  • Share with your group and invite clarifying questions.
  • Reveal the lie and explain the true story in more depth.

Activity 2

Speed greeting in breakout rooms

Rotate partners and aim for extended answers. Listen actively so you can introduce your partner to the whole group afterwards.

  • Which film, series, or book has influenced you recently?
  • If you could borrow a superpower for one day, what would you do with it?
  • Which era would you visit to witness history first-hand?
  • Share a fun fact classmates are unlikely to know.
  • Offer a quote that motivates you when learning.
Game time

Arab World Pub Quiz

Work as a team to predict the correct option. Use evidence or personal experiences to support your choice before checking the answer.

Question 1

The ancient city of Petra is in which country?

A) Egypt
B) Jordan
C) Lebanon
D) Syria

Petra, the "Rose City", is carved into sandstone cliffs in southern Jordan.

Question 2

What is the capital of Morocco?

A) Casablanca
B) Marrakesh
C) Rabat
D) Fes

Rabat is the political capital, even though Casablanca is Morocco's largest city.

Question 3

Which sea is famous for being so salty you can float easily?

A) The Red Sea
B) The Mediterranean Sea
C) The Dead Sea
D) The Arabian Sea

The Dead Sea's extreme salinity creates natural buoyancy for swimmers.

Question 4

The world's tallest building, the Burj Khalifa, is in which city?

A) Doha
B) Dubai
C) Riyadh
D) Abu Dhabi

Dubai in the United Arab Emirates has held the height record since 2009.

Question 5

The “Cedars of God” forest is in which country?

A) Lebanon
B) Syria
C) Jordan
D) Palestine

The ancient cedar forest near Bsharri, Lebanon is protected as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Question 6

What is the traditional art of beautiful handwriting called?

A) Calligraphy
B) Mosaic
C) Engraving
D) Illumination

Arabic calligraphy transforms written language into intricate art forms.

Question 7

In which country can you visit the Pyramids of Giza?

A) Sudan
B) Egypt
C) Algeria
D) Libya

The pyramids rise just outside Cairo, Egypt, along the Nile River valley.

Question 8

The Rub' al Khali desert is also known as the...

A) Great Sand Sea
B) Empty Quarter
C) White Desert
D) Black Desert

The Rub' al Khali, spanning the Arabian Peninsula, earns the nickname "Empty Quarter" for its vast dunes.

Question 9

What is the currency of the United Arab Emirates?

A) Riyal
B) Dinar
C) Dirham
D) Pound

The Emirati dirham (AED) has been the UAE's official currency since 1973.
