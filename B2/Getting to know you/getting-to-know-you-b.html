B1 English Lesson

Shared Passions: Discovering Common Ground

Discuss hobbies and personality, express preferences with nuance, and collaborate to find surprising connections.

Today's Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to...

  • Use a variety of phrases to express preferences (e.g., I'm keen on, I'd rather, I can't stand).
  • Use a wider range of vocabulary to describe hobbies and personality traits.
  • Make comparisons and justify opinions using more complex sentences.
  • Ask follow-up questions to gain a deeper understanding of your classmates' interests.
  • Collaborate in a group to discover common interests and present your findings.

Language Focus

Beyond "Like": Expressing Preferences

Let's upgrade our language. Instead of just "I like" or "I don't like," we can express our feelings with more detail and nuance.

Vocabulary Spectrum

How much do you enjoy it?

Positive Feelings

  • I'm passionate about... (+ ing)
  • I'm keen on... (+ ing)
  • I really enjoy... (+ ing)
  • I don't mind... (+ ing)

Negative Feelings

  • I can't stand... (+ ing)
  • I'm not a big fan of... (+ ing)
  • ... isn't really my thing.
  • I'd rather... (verb) than... (verb)
Activity 1 · Sentence Transformation

Let's practice the new vocabulary

How it works

Read the simple sentence. Then, rewrite it or say it aloud using one of the new phrases from the spectrum.

Example

Simple: “I don't like running.”

B1 Level: “I'm not a big fan of running.” or “Running isn't really my thing.” or "I can't stand running."

Your turn: "I like cooking on weekends."

Grammar Focus

Comparing Activities

To compare two activities, we can use prefer...to... or would rather...than...

  • I prefer drinking tea to drinking coffee. (verb-ing)
  • I'd rather listen to a podcast than read a book. (infinitive without 'to')

Activity 2 · Find Someone Who...

How to play

Step 1 · Get Your Card

Receive a list of characteristics (e.g., "Find someone who has recently taken up a new hobby").

Step 2 · Mingle & Ask

Walk around the room and ask your classmates questions to find people who match the descriptions.

Step 3 · Take Notes

When you find a match, write down their name and one extra piece of information.

Activity 2 · Prompts

Find someone who...

  • ...has recently taken up a new hobby. (What is it?)
  • ...prefers indoor activities to outdoor ones. (Why?)
  • ...thinks watching sports is more exciting than playing them. (Which sport?)
  • ...has given up a hobby they used to love. (What happened?)

Remember to use Present Perfect for experiences (Have you ever...?) and ask follow-up questions!

Activity 3

Discussion Time

In small groups, discuss the following statements. Do you agree or disagree? Make sure to justify your opinions.

Discussion Prompts

What's your opinion?

Quantity vs. Quality

Is it better to have many different hobbies, or to dedicate yourself to mastering just one? Why?

Hobby vs. Job

Some people say that turning a hobby into a job ruins the enjoyment. Do you agree? Why or why not?

Social vs. Solitary

Do you think the best hobbies are ones you can do with other people, or ones you can enjoy alone? Explain your reasoning.

The Dream Hobby

Describe a hobby you would love to try if you had unlimited time and money. What makes it so appealing?

Activity 4

Problem Solving: The Hobby Advisor

Your colleague, Alex, has just moved to the city. Alex is introverted but wants to meet a few people. They work long hours, so they need a hobby that is flexible. They find team sports too competitive. What should they try?

Activity 4 · Brainstorm

Share your advice for Alex

Your Task

In groups, brainstorm and recommend two hobbies for Alex. Be prepared to explain your choices.

Useful Phrases

“I think Alex should try... because it's...”
“Although... can be solitary, it's also a great way to...”
"Compared to team sports, ... is much less competitive."

Discuss & Justify

Consider the pros and cons of each suggestion. Why is your recommendation the best fit for Alex's personality and schedule?

Main Task · Part 1

Group Profile Interview

Goal

In groups of 3-4, find out what you have in common and what makes each of you unique.

Use the full range of language from today's lesson.

How it works

  • Interview each other about your pastimes, routines, and preferences.
  • Ask follow-up questions to understand the reasons *why*.
  • Use the next slide as a template to take notes on your group's profile.

Interview tips

  • "What do you usually do to unwind after a long day?"
  • "Have you ever tried a hobby that was completely out of your comfort zone?"
  • "Do you prefer activities that are stimulating or therapeutic?"
