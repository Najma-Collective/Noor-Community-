B2 English Review Lesson

Strategic Planning

From Partnerships to Project Proposals

Today's Goals

Talk About Experience

Use past tenses to describe an organization's background.

Discuss Potential

Use modals (might, could) to talk about possible partnerships.

Compare Scenarios

Use the second conditional to compare hypothetical options.

Pitch a Project

Combine all your skills to pitch a project to a potential donor.

Let's Talk!

A Successful Project

Think of a successful community project in Palestine.

  1. What past challenges have they faced?
  2. Who could they partner with to grow?
  3. If a donor offered them $50,000, what would they start?

Activity 1

Connecting Ideas

Let's combine vocabulary from different topics to make a strong point.

For example:

"Our last impact assessment showed a need for a new funding stream."

"A strong partnership could create synergy and lead to a better grant proposal."

Activity 1: Your Turn!

The Elevator Pitch

With a partner, create a short pitch for an imaginary NGO project. Your pitch must include:

  • Two words like: sustainability, mutual benefit
  • Two words like: deliverable, budget allocation

Activity 2

Past & Future

In your groups, discuss your NGO's story.

Part 1: The Past

Tell your group about a project your NGO has completed. What did you learn from it?

Part 2: The Future

Brainstorm a new project. What partnerships could you form, and what outcomes might you see?

Different Paths

Activity 3

We use the second conditional to talk about imaginary situations and their results.

For example:

"If we formed a partnership with a local community group, our stakeholder engagement would improve."

Activity 3: Role-Play

Expert & Novice

Partner A: New Coordinator

You have a simple project idea (e.g., a community clean-up day). Present it to your manager.

Partner B: Experienced Manager

Give advice by comparing two hypothetical partnership options using the second conditional.

e.g., "If you partnered with the municipality, you would get official support. But if you partnered with schools..."

Activity 4: The Big Decision

In your groups, discuss this scenario and choose the best strategy.

Your NGO has just finished a successful project. A donor will give you a large grant, but only if you form a major new partnership. Your options are:

A) A government agency.
B) A large international NGO.

Which do you choose, and why?

Main Task

Role-Play: The Donor Pitch

This is your chance to use all the language from our lessons in a realistic scenario.

The Pitch: Roles

In pairs, decide who is the NGO Representative and who is the Donor.

NGO Representative:

Your goal is to convince the donor. You must:

  1. Explain what your NGO has already achieved.
  2. Describe a partnership that might be successful.
  3. Explain two options for the funding using "If we..., we would..."

Donor:

Listen carefully, ask questions, and decide if you are convinced!

Main Task: Follow-Up

1. Report Back

Join another pair. The NGO representatives will report on their meetings. How did it go? What was the donor's biggest question?

2. Discuss

As a group of four, agree on one answer: "What is the single most important thing to do when pitching a project to a donor?"

Let's Reflect!

What is one new vocabulary word from today that you will try to use in your real life?

How confident do you feel now about pitching a project in English? (1=Not confident, 5=Very confident)
