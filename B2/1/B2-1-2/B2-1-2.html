Lesson Aims

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to...

  • Discuss opportunities and challenges for artisanal co-operatives in your community.
  • Compare funding options using a structured decision guide.
  • Use the second conditional to explain hypothetical advantages and disadvantages.
  • Justify a funding decision with clear reasons and relevant examples.
Pre-task A · 5 minutes

Artisanal Skills Confidence Check

In breakout rooms: Ask your partner(s) to rate their confidence from 1 (low) to 5 (high) in each artisanal skill. Encourage follow-up questions to uncover stories behind their scores.

  1. Traditional Embroidery (Tatreez): Creating complex, traditional patterns by hand.
  2. Natural Dyeing: Using local plants and materials to create colours for threads.
  3. Pattern Design & Curation: Researching and adapting historical patterns for modern use.
  4. Online Marketing: Photographing and selling artisanal products on social media or e-commerce sites.

Be ready to share one surprising insight about your partner with the class.

Pre-task B · Restrictive Practice · 7 minutes

Analyzing a Model Dialogue

Instructions: Read the short dialogue between Fatima and Yousef, who run an embroidery co-operative. Then, click a token and tap the correct cell in the table to summarize their discussion.

Fatima: I’ve been reviewing our funding options. The “Cultural Heritage Grant” from the EU looks promising. If we got that grant, we would have a much bigger budget for our new training workshop.

Yousef: True, but the application process is incredibly complex. We would need to hire a consultant just to complete the paperwork. What about the “Local Artisans Fund”?

Fatima: It’s a great local option, and the application is simple. But if we chose that fund, the amount would be much smaller. We would only be able to buy new materials, not expand the workshop.

Yousef: I see. So, a bigger budget with more bureaucracy, or a smaller, simpler grant. Given our goal is expansion, I think we should aim for the EU grant. If we spent the money on a consultant now, it would pay off in the long run.

Pre-task B · Freer Practice · 6 minutes

Exploring the Consequences

Scenario: The “Global Heritage Fund” offers your embroidery co-op a $100,000 grant. The catch: you must switch from traditional hand-stitching to using sewing machines to increase production. Your co-op is famous for its authentic, handmade quality.

In breakout rooms, discuss:

  • If we accepted the grant and used machines, how would our customers and community react?
  • If we refused the grant, how would it affect our co-op's future?
  • What would be the biggest challenge if we accepted the money?
  • What decision would you make, and why?

Pre-task C · Freer Practice · 6 minutes

Which Project Has More Merit?

In breakout rooms: Read both proposals carefully. Use the rubric to decide which project deserves funding. Bring the new vocabulary into your discussion.

Proposal A · “Tatreez for Tomorrow”

Deliverable: A free 6-month embroidery training program for 20 young women. Budget allocation focuses on master artisan salaries and high-quality materials. A measurable outcome is 80% of graduates being able to recreate a complex historical pattern.

Proposal B · “Heritage Threads Online”

Funding stream: A mix of international and local grants. Deliverable: An e-commerce website and marketing training for 50 established artisans. Measurable outcome: A 30% increase in online sales for each participant within one year.

Rubric: Which proposal has a clearer (1) deliverable, (2) budget allocation idea, and (3) measurable outcome?

Task Preparation

The Funding Committee Brief

Rich Context: You are the selection committee for the “Palestinian Heritage Fund,” a new NGO dedicated to supporting cultural preservation. You have a total of $50,000 to award for one flagship project this year. Your goal is to choose the project that offers the most sustainable and meaningful impact for Palestinian artisans. Two excellent proposals have made it to the final round.

Your Task (10 minutes in breakout rooms):

  1. Read the two project outlines on the next slide.
  2. As a committee, score each project using the rubric below.
  3. Use your scores to decide which single project you will fund.
  4. Prepare to justify your decision to the board of directors, using the second conditional to explore potential impacts (e.g., “If we funded Project A, it would strengthen traditional skills, but if we chose Project B, it would create more immediate income.”).
Evaluation Category Guiding Questions Project A Score (1-5) Project B Score (1-5)
1. Clarity of Vision How clear is the project's main goal? Is it easy to understand the problem they are solving?
2. Community Impact How significant is the potential benefit to the community? Will it create meaningful, positive change?
3. Realistic Plan Does the plan seem practical and achievable? Are the steps logical and well-thought-out?
4. Long-Term Value Will the benefits last after the project ends? Does it create sustainable skills or opportunities?
Task · Breakout Discussion

Choose the Project

Discuss these outlines using the rubric. Which one deserves the $50,000 grant?

Project A · “Hebron Hills Heritage Hub”

Goal: To establish a physical training hub in a village near Hebron to teach traditional Tatreez and natural dyeing to 30 young women, ensuring the survival of endangered local patterns.

Plan: This 12-month, in-person program focuses on deep, hands-on skill-building. The budget prioritizes renting a workshop space, paying salaries for two master artisans, and purchasing authentic, high-quality materials. The key deliverable is a documented portfolio of revived patterns for each participant and a final community exhibition. The project emphasizes cultural preservation and creating a new generation of highly skilled artisans to carry the traditions forward.

Project B · “Gaza Digital Souk”

Goal: To build a professional e-commerce platform and deliver digital marketing training to 75 established female artisans in Gaza, connecting them directly to international buyers.

Plan: This 8-month, primarily online program focuses on economic empowerment. The budget is allocated to platform development, professional product photography, and targeted social media advertising campaigns. The key measurable outcome is to increase the average monthly income of each participant by 50% within a year. The project emphasizes creating immediate, sustainable income streams and giving artisans the modern business skills they need to be independent.

Reporting

Presenting to the Board of Directors

  1. Each “Funding Committee” (your group) will present a one-minute justification for the project they selected.
  2. As you listen to other groups, note which arguments use the second conditional effectively to explain their reasoning.
  3. After all groups present, we will vote as a class on which project should officially receive the funding.
