B2 English Lesson

NGO Strategy: From Planning to Partnership

Practice discussing strategy and forming collaborations.

Today's Goals

Connect Vocabulary

Combine language about environmental issues and partnerships.

Use Past Tenses

Summarize the background of a project or problem.

Speculate on Outcomes

Use modals (might, could, may) to discuss future possibilities.

Negotiate a Partnership

Role-play a professional call to propose a joint venture.

Let's Talk!

Discuss with a partner.

1. What makes a community project in your area successful?

2. Do successful projects usually need partnerships? Why?

3. After an NGO finishes a report, what is their next step?

Activity 1

Connecting Ideas

With a partner, create a short dialogue between two NGO managers.

Your dialogue must include at least two words from each list.

Environment

  • sustainability
  • conservation
  • stakeholder
  • impact assessment

Partnerships

  • synergy
  • mutual benefit
  • joint venture
  • shared resources

Activity 2

Project Stories

Think about a team project you have been part of (at work, university, or in your community).

Tell your group the story. Use past tenses to explain the background.

Guiding Questions:

  • What was the main goal?
  • What challenges were you facing?
  • What had you planned before you started?
  • What happened in the end?

Future Solutions

Activity 3

Your Task (in pairs):

Choose a local environmental problem you know well (e.g., waste, pollution).

Brainstorm solutions. For each solution, describe the potential positive outcomes using might, could, or may.

"If we started a community recycling program, we could reduce waste."

Activity 4: The Tech Partnership

An NGO has identified a major problem with the local carbon footprint. They want to propose a joint venture with a tech company.

In your groups, brainstorm a project proposal. What kind of project could they propose?

The Main Task

Role-Play: Proposing a Partnership

Time to use all the language in a professional negotiation.

Scenario: A Cross-Border Collaboration

Person A: NGO in Hebron

Call Person B. Summarize the problem your NGO has identified using past tenses. Propose a partnership and explain the positive outcomes using might, could, may.

Person B: Consultancy in Amman

You receive the call. Listen to the proposal, ask questions to clarify details, and negotiate a possible next step (e.g., "We could schedule a follow-up call...").

Follow-Up Task

1. Report Back

Join another pair. Summarize the partnership you just negotiated.

Explain the project, the potential synergy, and the next step you agreed upon.

2. Discuss

After you both report back, discuss: Which of the two proposed partnerships sounds more promising for long-term sustainability?
