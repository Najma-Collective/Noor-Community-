The Language of Planning

Core Concepts

Strategic Planning Essentials

Stakeholder

A person, group, or organization who can affect or be affected by a project.

Sustainability

The ability for a project's positive outcomes to continue after the initial funding or effort has ended.

Impact Assessment

A process of evaluating the likely positive and negative effects of a proposed project.

Mission Statement

A formal summary of the aims and values of an organization.

Deliverable

A tangible product or service produced as the result of a project.

Analyzing a Project's History

Listening Model

Fatima & Yousef Discuss Recycling

Task: What is the main problem, and what two options are they considering?

Fatima: Okay, so the municipal report came out last week. It showed that while we were running the awareness campaign last year, recycling rates hadn't improved as much as we'd hoped. The main issue they identified was contamination in the bins.

Yousef: I see. So, we have two options: invest in expensive sorting technology at the plant, or run a new series of targeted community workshops. For me, the choice is clear. The key to long-term sustainability is direct engagement. We need to focus on the stakeholders themselves.

Presenting Data with Impact

Use pronunciation to make your message clear and persuasive.

Pronunciation: Using Stress to Emphasize Data

When we present data from a report, we stress the most important word to make our point. This changes the focus and the meaning of the sentence.

Noticing Task

Listen to your teacher say the sentences below. The stressed word is in bold. How does the meaning change?

A. The report showed a twenty percent increase in water usage. (Focus is on the number itself.)

B. The report showed a twenty percent increase in water usage. (Focus is on the direction of change.)

  2. Analyze the two proposed strategies and choose the most effective, sustainable option.
